Immigration to Australia (1980-2022)

Description of the choropleth map goes here.

There is an overall upwards trend in immigration, with the numbers steadily increasing from around 150,000 in 1980 to a peak of nearly 600,000 before 2020. A significant drop occurs in 2019, coinciding with the global COVID-19 pandemic, which drastically affected migration flows due to travel restrictions. However, by 2022, the number of immigrants rebounds strongly, exceeding pre-pandemic levels.

Asia has been the dominant source of immigrants to Australia, particularly after the 2000s, as seen by the dark green shades representing high immigration levels. Europe, once a major contributor, shows a decline in immigration over the years, while Africa and Oceania maintain moderate but steady trends. North America shows a slight increase in recent decades, and South America remains the least significant contributor, though it has experienced a gradual rise. The pandemic's impact is also evident, with immigration levels falling sharply in 2020 across all continents before recovering by 2022.
